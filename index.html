Topic 1: Real Numbers

Foundation Topic

What Are Real Numbers?

Real numbers include ALL the numbers you can think of on the number line. They are divided into two main groups:

Rational Numbers

Numbers that CAN be written as a fraction (a/b where b is not 0)

  • Integers: -3, 0, 5
  • Fractions: 1/2, -3/4
  • Terminating decimals: 0.5, 3.25
  • Repeating decimals: 0.333..., 0.142857...

Irrational Numbers

Numbers that CANNOT be written as a fraction

  • Non-repeating, non-terminating decimals
  • Square roots of non-perfect squares: √2, √3, √5
  • Pi (π) = 3.14159265...
  • e = 2.71828...

Key Skill: Identifying Rational vs Irrational

1

Check if it's a perfect square root: √4 = 2, √9 = 3, √16 = 4, √25 = 5, √36 = 6, √49 = 7, √64 = 8, √81 = 9, √100 = 10

2

If the square root is NOT a perfect square, it's irrational: √2, √3, √5, √7, √10, √11, etc.

3

Fractions and terminating/repeating decimals are ALWAYS rational

Key Skill: Estimating Square Roots

Example: Where is √50 on the number line?

Step 1: Find the perfect squares around 50

√49 = 7 and √64 = 8

Step 2: Since 50 is between 49 and 64, √50 is between 7 and 8

Step 3: 50 is closer to 49, so √50 ≈ 7.07

Topic 2: Laws of Exponents

Core Skills

The Exponent Rules

Exponents are shortcuts for repeated multiplication. Here are the rules you MUST know:

Product Rule

xa · xb = xa+b

x5 · x3 = x8

Same base? ADD the exponents!

Quotient Rule

xa ÷ xb = xa-b

x7 ÷ x4 = x3

Same base? SUBTRACT the exponents!

Power Rule

(xa)b = xa·b

(x3)4 = x12

Power of a power? MULTIPLY exponents!

Power of a Product

(xy)a = xaya

(2y)3 = 8y3

Distribute the exponent to each factor!

Negative Exponents

x-a = 1/xa

5m-2 = 5/m2

Negative exponent? Flip to denominator!

Zero Exponent

x0 = 1

50 = 1

Anything to the zero power equals 1!

Worked Example

Simplify: (2y3)4

Step 1: Apply power of a product rule - distribute the 4

= 24 · (y3)4

Step 2: Calculate 24 = 16

Step 3: Apply power rule: (y3)4 = y3×4 = y12

Answer: 16y12

Common Mistakes to Avoid!

  • Wrong: x5 · x3 = x15 → Right: x8 (ADD, don't multiply!)
  • Wrong: 5m-2 = 1/(5m2) → Right: 5/m2 (only m has the negative exponent)
  • Wrong: (2y)3 = 2y3 → Right: 8y3 (raise 2 to the power too!)

Topic 3: Scientific Notation & Radicals

Application Skills

Scientific Notation

Scientific notation is used to write very large or very small numbers in a compact form:

a × 10n where 1 ≤ a < 10

Large Numbers (Positive Exponent)

4,500,000 = 4.5 × 106

Count how many places you move the decimal LEFT

Small Numbers (Negative Exponent)

0.000072 = 7.2 × 10-5

Count how many places you move the decimal RIGHT

Key Skill: Operations with Scientific Notation

Multiply: (4 × 105) × (3 × 10-2)

Step 1: Multiply the coefficients: 4 × 3 = 12

Step 2: Add the exponents: 5 + (-2) = 3

Step 3: Combine: 12 × 103

Step 4: Adjust to proper form: 1.2 × 104

Answer: 1.2 × 104

Simplifying Radicals (Square Roots)

To simplify a square root, find the largest perfect square factor:

Simplify: √72

Step 1: Find the largest perfect square that divides 72

72 = 36 × 2 (36 is a perfect square!)

Step 2: Split the radical: √72 = √36 × √2

Step 3: Simplify: √36 = 6

Answer: 6√2

Perfect Squares to Memorize:

1, 4, 9, 16, 25, 36, 49, 64, 81, 100, 121, 144

Topic 4: Equivalent Expressions

Algebra Foundations

Factoring with GCF

Factoring means finding what's common and pulling it out:

Factor: 12x + 18

Step 1: Find the GCF of 12 and 18

12 = 2 × 2 × 3 and 18 = 2 × 3 × 3

GCF = 2 × 3 = 6

Step 2: Divide each term by 6

12x ÷ 6 = 2x and 18 ÷ 6 = 3

Answer: 6(2x + 3)

The Distributive Property

Distributing means multiplying the outside term by EACH term inside:

a(b + c) = ab + ac

Expand: 3(2x - 5) + 4(x + 2)

Step 1: Distribute 3: 3(2x - 5) = 6x - 15

Step 2: Distribute 4: 4(x + 2) = 4x + 8

Step 3: Combine like terms: 6x + 4x = 10x and -15 + 8 = -7

Answer: 10x - 7

FOIL Method (Multiplying Binomials)

FOIL stands for: First, Outer, Inner, Last

Multiply: (x + 4)(x - 3)

First: x · x = x2

Outer: x · (-3) = -3x

Inner: 4 · x = 4x

Last: 4 · (-3) = -12

Combine: x2 - 3x + 4x - 12

Answer: x2 + x - 12

Topic 5: Equations & Inequalities

Problem Solving

Solving Multi-Step Equations

Goal: Get the variable ALONE on one side!

Solve: 3(x - 2) + 4 = 2x + 5

Step 1: Distribute: 3x - 6 + 4 = 2x + 5

Step 2: Combine like terms: 3x - 2 = 2x + 5

Step 3: Get x terms on one side: 3x - 2x = 5 + 2

Answer: x = 7

Special Cases: No Solution vs Infinite Solutions

No Solution

When you get a FALSE statement like:

0 = 5 or 3 = 7

This means NO value of x works!

Infinite Solutions

When you get a TRUE statement like:

0 = 0 or 5 = 5

This means ALL values of x work!

Solving Inequalities

Inequalities are like equations, but with ONE crucial difference:

FLIP THE SIGN when you multiply or divide by a NEGATIVE number!

Solve: -2x + 5 > 11

Step 1: Subtract 5 from both sides: -2x > 6

Step 2: Divide by -2 AND FLIP THE SIGN!

Answer: x < -3

Solving x2 = k and x3 = k

Quadratic: x2 = k

x2 = 81

x = ±√81

x = 9 or x = -9

Remember BOTH solutions!

Cubic: x3 = k

x3 = -27

x = ∛(-27)

x = -3

Cube roots CAN be negative!
